types of database

  • Most of us encounter several activities every day that involve some interaction with a database. For example, traditional database applications
    • Bank: we go to the bank to deposit or withdraw funds.
    • Hotel: we make a hotel reservation.
    • Credit hours: registers of students enrolled in a university and grades in their exams
    • Airline: we make a airline reservation.
    • Computerized Library: we access a computerized library catalog to search for a bibliographic item.
    • Online Shopping: we purchase something such as a book, toy, or computer.
    • Supermarket: Even purchasing items at a supermarket often automatically updates the database that holds the inventory of grocery items.
    All these databases are called traditional database applications in which most of the information that is stored and accessed is either textual or numeric.
  • multimedia databases: is a new media technology has made it possible to store images, audio clips, and video streams digitally.
  • Geographic information systems (GIS): can store and analyze maps, weather data, and satellite images.
  • Data warehouses and online analytical processing (OLAP) systems: are used in many companies to extract and analyze useful business information from very large databases to support decision making.
  • Real-time and active database technology: is used to control industrial and manufacturing processes.
  • World Wide Web (WWW) database search techniques: are being applied to WWW to improve the search for information that is needed by users browsing the Internet.
